How Your Computational Thinking is Assessed
  • Decomposition: Breaking down the task into logical, manageable steps. For example, planning each move the robot needs to reach the apple.
  • Pattern Recognition: Identifying and using repeated movement patterns or sequences in your solution.
  • Abstraction: Simplifying or generalizing your actions to create more efficient strategies, such as using loops or reusable blocks.
  • Algorithm Design: Creating clear and efficient sequences of coding blocks to solve the problem.
  • Debugging: Adjusting your strategy after mistakes, such as fixing errors or trying new approaches when something doesn't work.

Each time you complete an item, an AI reviews your actions and provides feedback on these skills.
